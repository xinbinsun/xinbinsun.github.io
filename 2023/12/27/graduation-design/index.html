图片大面积修复(思路)：

To restore the 20 images with black stripes based on the content of the normal image (complete image taken every 21 frames) and to ensure that the repaired part conforms to the dynamic effect of the frame, you can consider using a technique known as video inpainting. Inpainting is a process that involves filling in missing or damaged parts of an image or video.

Steps:

  1. Identify Black Stripes:
    • Use image processing techniques to identify the black stripes in each frame. This may involve thresholding, edge detection, or other computer vision methods.
  2. Generate Masks:
    • Create masks corresponding to the identified black stripes in each frame. These masks will be used to inpaint the missing regions.
  3. Track Dynamic Changes:
    • Track the dynamic changes in the scene from the complete images (captured every 21 frames). This could involve optical flow or other motion tracking techniques to understand how objects move between the complete images.
  4. Content Transfer:
    • Transfer content from the corresponding complete image to the frames with black stripes. Use information from the dynamic changes tracked in step 3 to adjust the transferred content based on the motion in each frame.
  5. Inpainting:
    • Apply video inpainting techniques to fill in the missing regions based on the generated masks and transferred content. There are various inpainting algorithms available, and deep learning-based methods such as convolutional neural networks (CNNs) can be effective.
  6. Temporal Consistency:
    • Ensure temporal consistency by considering the information from neighboring frames. This can be achieved by using information from previous and subsequent frames to refine the inpainting results.
  7. Fine-Tuning:
    • Optionally, fine-tune the results to improve the visual quality and ensure that the inpainted regions seamlessly blend with the rest of the frame.

Tools and Libraries:

  • OpenCV: Use OpenCV for image processing tasks and handling video frames.
  • Deep Learning Frameworks: Consider using deep learning frameworks like TensorFlow or PyTorch for inpainting tasks. You may find pre-trained models or train a model on your specific data.

Notes:

  • The success of this approach depends on the complexity of the scenes and the effectiveness of the inpainting algorithm. Experiment with different techniques and parameters to achieve the best results.
  • It’s recommended to work on a small subset of frames initially to validate and fine-tune the approach before applying it to the entire video sequence.

实现流程

Video Inpainting: Video inpainting is a technique that can be used to restore missing or corrupted parts of video frames. It takes into account the temporal continuity of the video, which can be very helpful in your case as the images have a high frame rate of 50 frames per second. There are several deep learning-based methods for video inpainting that have been proposed in recent years, such as Copy-and-Paste Networks for Deep Video Inpainting1 and Deep Flow-Guided Video Inpainting1.

Multi-Frame Image Fusion: Multi-frame image fusion is a technique that combines information from multiple images to create a single image that is more informative and complete than any of the input images. This technique can be used to fuse the information from the normal images and the missing images to restore the missing parts. There are several methods for multi-frame image fusion, such as the DeepFuse: A Deep Unsupervised Approach for Exposure Fusion with Extreme Exposure Image Pairs1.

Optical Flow Estimation: Optical flow estimation can be used to estimate the motion pattern between two consecutive frames, which can be used to guide the inpainting process. There are several deep learning-based methods for optical flow estimation, such as PWC-Net: CNNs for Optical Flow Using Pyramid, Warping, and Cost Volume1.

By combining these methods, you can create a system that takes into account the continuity of the images, the missing images at different positions, and the normal images with a 20 frame interval.

知识储备

三维拍摄为什么会产生格雷码和相移码形成黑色条纹遮挡图像？

在三维扫描应用中，结构光重建的方案有多种，其中条纹法是应用比较广泛的一类方法](https://zhuanlan.zhihu.com/p/81731606)。条纹法通过投射一组时序变化的条纹图案，来辅助完成立体匹配和视差计算、或者深度的直接计算。根据不同的条纹图案，衍生出了一系列方法，如格雷码等二值编码方案、正弦条纹的相移法、格雷码+相移法、彩色条纹法等等。

格雷码和相移法结合使用的方式，由于被扫描物体的反射率不均匀，背景强度和散焦等引起的干扰，导致捕获的格雷码条纹图像在黑白转换边界中并不总是很清晰，这可能会引入错误的解码相位顺序并导致 2π展开阶段的相位误差。

对于格雷码来说，明暗交接处变换更少，解码很重要的环节就是二值化，一般情况下全黑和全亮是不容易出错的，因为在实际应用中投影交界处是渐变的，阈值很难确定。这就是为什么在三维拍摄中会产生格雷码和相移码形成的黑色条纹遮挡图像的原因。

如何解决这个问题？

解决三维拍摄中格雷码和相移码形成黑色条纹遮挡图像的问题，有一些方法可以尝试：

  1. 优化编码图案：通过对投射的编码图案的分析和设计，确定适用条件并优化了格雷码的编码周期和相移条纹周期1。
  2. 格雷码与相移相结合的编码策略：根据格雷码和相移法的编码数学特性，设计正反格雷码灰度条纹图案，找到具有相同编码的区域作为一个初始编码周期。采用四步相移法使格雷码图案的最小周期为相移图案周期4倍，依次投射四幅相移图案使得每个编码区域被进一步连续细分偏4次后光栅条纹图案与未偏移时的图案重合。
  3. 增加两张图：一张全黑图，一张全亮图。这是传统格雷码结合相移技术需要额外投射两幅全黑和全白图案的原因。
  4. 去除全0和全1的编码：让每个编码至少含有一个0和一个1。但这可能会破坏格雷码的稳定性。

我想将三维扫描中受影响的图象恢复成完整图象，有哪些方法？

在三维扫描中，如果图像受到了影响，例如由于格雷码和相移码形成的黑色条纹遮挡，那么可以通过以下方法尝试恢复成完整图像：

  1. 特征提取与特征描述：这通常默认使用SIFT算子。
  2. 特征匹配：对每一对相片进行特征匹配。
  3. 几何滤波：应用几何滤波剔除错误匹配点。
  4. 建立匹配点集：建立匹配点集（tracks）。
  5. 恢复相机参数：应用SfM方法恢复相机的内参和外参。
  6. 三角测量：由三角测量（triangulation）得到三维点坐标。
  7. 全局优化：然后使用光束法平差（bundle adjustment）进行全局优化。

在三维拍摄中，如果二维图像受到格雷码和相移码形成的黑色条纹遮挡，可以尝试以下方法来恢复：

  1. 优化编码图案：根据格雷码和相移法的编码数学特性，设计正反格雷码灰度条纹图案，找到具有相同编码的区域作为一个初始编码周期。采用四步相移法使格雷码图案的最小周期为相移图案周期4倍，依次投射四幅相移图案使得每个编码区域被进一步连续细分。
  2. 增加两张图：一张全黑图，一张全亮图。这是传统格雷码结合相移技术需要额外投射两幅全黑和全白图案的原因。
